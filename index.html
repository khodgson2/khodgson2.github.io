The Deep Sea

The deep sea is a dark, frigid environment in which nutrients are scarce. Scavengers subsist on marine snow, organic detritus that drifts into the depths from shallow water. Bioluminescence allows some creatures to attract prey in the darkness. Some bacteria derive energy from the chemicals found in hydrothermal vents, cold seeps, and brine lakes, allowing ecosystems to form in these harsh environments.

Source: Smithsonian Institution
